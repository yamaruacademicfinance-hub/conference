We examine human capital's role in the gap in the quality of financial services in high- versus low income neighborhoods. Using a near comprehensive panel of over 350,000 U.S. mortgage loan officers, we document large and persistent differences in loan officer productivity and performance. We find that firms' hiring, promotion, and retention policies disproportionately assign inexperienced and low performing employees to branches serving low income customers. These 'bad apples' are more likely to commit misconduct, and to write bad loans that borrowers cannot afford. These labor market factors are important in explaining the observed income based disparities in the quality of financial services.

Disparities in Consumer Credit: The Role of Loan Officers in the FinTech Era
Authors: Erica Jiang; University of Southern California), Yeonjoon Lee; Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond), Will Liu; City University of Hong Kong)
📄 PDF

1）マイノリティのローンオフィサーは金融機関、特に小規模銀行やシャドウバンクに少ない、（2）マイノリティのローンオフィサーが多い地方市場では、人種による住宅ローンの拒絶ギャップが小さい、（3）この関連性は、アルゴリズムによる引き受けをより採用している市場では著しく弱い、という事実である。我々は、これらの観測の主要なメカニズムは、融資担当者が借り手との人種的な近接性に応じて裁量権を行使することであることを示す。具体的には、マイノリティの借り手からの融資申し込みは、同じ人種のマイノリティの融資担当者が処理すれば、拒絶される可能性が低くなるということである。しかし、このような人種的近接性の有益な効果は、多くの政府系ローンプログラムで義務付けられているアルゴリズムによる査定によって著しく弱められている。

Using new administrative data on all registered mortgage loan officers in the U.S. linked to individual loan applications that they process, we document three facts: (1) minority loan officers are under represented in financial institutions, especially in small banks and shadow banks; (2) local markets with higher shares of minority loan officers are associated with lower racial mortgage rejection gap; (3) this association is significantly weaker in markets with more adoption of algorithmic underwriting. We show that a key mechanism for these observations is that loan officers exercise discretion according to their racial proximity with borrowers. Specifically, loan applications from minority borrowers are less likely to be rejected if they are processed by minority loan officers of the same race. However, this beneficial effect of racial proximity is significantly weakened by the use of algorithmic underwriting, which is required by many government loan programs.

What Problem Do Intermediaries Solve? Evidence From Real Estate Markets
Authors: Darren Aiello; Brigham Young University), Mark Garmaise; University of California (Los Angeles), Taylor Nadauld; Brigham Young University)
📄 PDF

我々は、検索摩擦のある市場における仲介の役割を研究する。不動産業者がリードを生成するために使用するオンラインプラットフォームのデータを用いて、潜在的なリスティングのランダムな順序付けから生じる外生的な仲介の注意を確認する。仲介者の注目度が高ければ高いほど、その仲介者に物件が掲載される確率が高くなり、早期売却の確率と取引価格の上昇につながる。しかし、隣接する物件の掲載確率と取引確率は、仲介者の注目度が高いほど低下する。これらの結果は、仲介の探索理論を支持する因果的証拠を提供し、データで観察された内生的相関とは大きく異なり、この市場におけるエージェントは、情報の非対称性を低減するというよりも、主に探索を促進するために機能していることを示すものである。

We study the role of intermediation in markets with search frictions. Using data from an online platform used by real estate agents to generate leads, we identify exogenous intermediary attention arising from the randomized ordering of potential listings. Higher intermediary attention increases the probability of listing with an agent and leads to a higher probability of a quick sale and a higher transaction price. The listing and transaction probabilities of neighboring properties, however, decrease in intermediary attention. These results provide causal evidence in favor of search theories of intermediation, contrast sharply with endogenous correlations observed in the data, and indicate that agents in this market serve mainly to facilitate search rather than to reduce information asymmetries.
