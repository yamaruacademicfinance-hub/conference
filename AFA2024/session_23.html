When do Treasuries Earn the Convenience Yield? --- A Hedging Perspective
Authors: Viral Acharya (New York University), Toomas Laarits (New York University)

米国債の利便性利回りは、安全資産のヘッジの観点と整合的な特性を示している。利便性利回りは、米国債のリターンと株式市場のリターンの共分散が大きい場合に低くなる傾向がある。この効果は、金利水準とVIXをコントロールし、金融危機をコントロールしてもロバストである。株式と債券の共分散を分解すると、利便性利回りと株式と債券の共分散の負の関係は、リスク・フリー・レートと株式リターンの共分散、利便性利回りと株式リターンの共分散の両方によってもたらされているが、定量的には米国の信用リスク・プレミアムの共分散によってもたらされている。国債の株式市場との共分散は、他の債券マネー類似資産においても安全プレミアムを低下させる。利便性利回りは、米国債や他の債券類似資産のヘッジ特性を低下させるインフレ期待の高まりによって悪影響を受け、金などの代替資産へのスイッチングを誘発するという証拠を示す。最後に、米国債の利便性利回りが他の安全な国債よりも相対的に高いのは、米国株と国債の共分散の値が低い場合である。

We document that the convenience yield of US Treasuries exhibits properties that are consistent with a hedging perspective of safe assets. The convenience yield tends to be low when the covariance of Treasury returns with the aggregate stock market returns is high. This effect is robust to controlling for the level of interest rates and the VIX, as well as controlling for the financial crisis. We decompose the aggregate stock-bond covariance and find that the negative relationship between convenience yields and stock-bond covariance is driven both by the covariance of the risk-free rate and stock returns, as well as of the covariance of convenience yields with stock returns, but quantitatively less so by the covariance of the US credit risk premium. Treasury covariance with the stock market reduces the safety premium also in other fixed-income money-like assets. We provide evidence that the convenience yield is adversely affected by heightened inflation expectations that erode the hedging properties of US Treasuries and other fixed-income money-like assets, and that induce a switch to alternatives such as gold. Finally, the relative convenience yield of US Treasuries over other safe government bonds increases with low values of the US stock-bond covariance.

This paper presents a dynamic model of the Treasury market, accounting for recent disruptions. We investigate the impact of various shocks on repo rates and Treasury yields and examine policy implications. Our findings highlight the crucial role of the reserves-to-outstanding Treasury securities ratio as a predictor of market disruptions and emphasize the importance of central bank balance sheet policies in maintaining stability. The model offers valuable insights for policymakers and serves as a foundation for future research on regulation and policy interventions in government securities markets.

The Imperfect Intermediation of Money-Like Assets
Authors: Jeremy Stein (Harvard University), Jonathan Wallen (Harvard University)
📄 PDF

我々は、貨幣類似資産の仲介における驚くべき摩擦を記録している。2022年3月から2023年1月にかけて、1ヵ月物国庫短期証券は連邦準備制度理事会（FRB）のリバース・レポよりも29bps低いリターンを示した。このようなマネー・ライク・レートの大きなスプレッドは、セグメンテーションとマネー・ファンドによる非弾力的な代替によるものであることを示す。単純な反実仮想として、マネー・ファンドがマネー類似資産間で弾力的に代替していたならば、スプレッドは19bps小さくなっていたことを示す。投資家による非弾力的な代替は、最も単純で、最もリスクが低く、最も透明性の高い資産市場であっても、「裁定取引」の一般的な限界である。

We document a surprising amount of friction in the intermediation of money-like assets. From March 2022 to January 2023, 1-month T-bills returned 29 bps less than reverse repo with the Federal Reserve. We show that this large spread in money-like rates is due to segmentation and inelastic substitution by money funds. In a simple counterfactual, we show that if the money funds had elastically substituted between money-like assets, then the spread would have been 19 bps smaller. Inelastic substitution by investors is a general limit of 'arbitrage', even for the simplest, lowest-risk and most transparent asset markets.

We use equity returns to construct a time-varying measure of what we call the zero-beta rate: the expected return of a stock portfolio orthogonal to the stochastic discount factor. In contrast to safe rates, the zero-beta rate fits the aggregate Euler equation remarkably well. It has a large and volatile spread with respect to the safe rate. This spread responds to monetary policy shocks, which move zero-beta and safe rates in opposite directions. We claim that the zero-beta rate is the correct intertemporal price and that the safe rate primarily reflects the behavior of a convenience yield on safe assets.
