Is Sustainable Investing Sustainable?

Sustainability or Greenwashing: Evidence from the Asset Market for Industrial Pollution
Authors: Ran Duchin (Boston College), Janet Gao (Georgetown University), Qiping Xu (University of Illinois)
📄 PDF

我々は、汚染プラントの資産市場について研究している。企業は環境リスクの発生後、汚染工場を売却する。しかし、売却後も汚染レベルは低下しない。買い手は、環境圧力が弱く、売り手とのサプライチェーン関係や合弁事業を持つ企業である。売り手は、その後の電話会議で自社の持続可能な方針を強調し、より多くの汚染工場を売却することで高いリターンを得、より高いESG評価と低いコンプライアンス・コストの恩恵を受ける。全体として、資産市場によって、企業は汚染レベルに実質的な影響を与えることなく、環境に優しいと認識される方法で境界線を引き直すことができ、取引から大きな利益を得ることができる。

We study the asset market for pollutive plants. Firms divest pollutive plants following environmental risk incidents. However, pollution levels do not decline after divesting. The buyers are firms facing weaker environmental pressures, with supply chain relationships or joint ventures with the sellers. The sellers highlight their sustainable policies in subsequent conference calls, earn higher returns as they sell more pollutive plants, and benefit from higher ESG ratings and lower compliance costs. Overall, the asset market allows firms to redraw their boundaries in a manner perceived as environmentally friendly without real consequences for pollution levels and with substantial gains from trade.

Why Do Investors Pay Higher Fees for Sustainable Investments? An Experiment in Five European Countries
Authors: Daniel Engler), Gunnar Gutsche), Paul Smeets (University of Amsterdam)
📄 PDF

持続可能な投資手数料に対する投資家の感度を高めるものは何か？欧州5カ国の個人投資家を対象に、大規模なオンライン実験を行った。投資家の手数料感応度には主に2つの要因がある。第一に、投資家は社会的選好から、サステナブル投資により高い手数料を支払うことを望むかもしれない。良いことをするために支払うのである。第二に、金融リテラシーの低い投資家は、サステナブル投資の手数料の高さがリターンに大きな影響を与えることを理解していない可能性がある。我々は、5カ国すべてにおいて、社会的選好が個人のサステナブル投資行動において重要な役割を果たしていることを発見したが、それは投資家が手数料に反応するかどうかを説明するものではない。むしろ、金融リテラシーの低い投資家が高い手数料を支払う傾向が強い。これらの結果は、金融商品の開発や、金融リテラシーの低い個人の潜在的利用にとって重要な意味を持つ。

What drives investors’ sensitivity to sustainable investment fees? We conduct a large-scale online experiment with samples of individual investors in five European countries. We explore two main channels for investors’ sensitivities to fees. First, investors might be willing to pay higher fees on sustainable investments because of their social preferences. They pay to do good. Second, investors with low financial literacy might not understand that higher fees on sustainable investments have a large influence on their net returns. We find that social preferences play an important role in individual sustainable investment behavior in all five countries, but they do not explain whether investors react to fees. Rather, investors with low financial literacy are most prone to paying high fees. These results have important implications for the development of financial products and potential exploitation of individuals with low financial literacy.

The CO2 Question: Technical Progress and the Climate Crisis
Authors: Patrick Bolton (Columbia University), Marcin Kacperczyk (Imperial College (London University), Moritz Wiedemann (Imperial College (London University)
📄 PDF

企業はグリーンな研究開発に傾倒しているのだろうか。また、企業行動、特に炭素排出量はグリーンな技術進歩の影響を受けているのだろうか。グローバルな特許出願と企業の財務報告に基づき、企業のグリーンとブラウンの研究開発活動と、炭素排出削減におけるその効果を分析する。その結果、褐色企業によるグリーンR&Dが限定的であるという一貫した証拠が得られた。炭素排出量の多い革新的企業は、茶色の研究開発により多く取り組み、グリーンの研究開発にはあまり取り組んでいない。グリーンR&Dの割合が着実に増加しているにもかかわらず、グリーン・イノベーションが、1)イノベーションを起こしている企業、2)同じセクターの非イノベーション企業、3)他のセクターの企業、4)国全体の将来の炭素排出量を削減するという証拠はほとんどない。短期的（グリーン特許出願から1年後）、中期的（出願から3年後、5年後）のいずれにおいても、直接排出量、間接排出量は、全てのセクター、世界中でグリーン・イノベーションによる有意な影響を受けていない。

Are companies gravitating towards green R&D and is corporate behavior, in particular carbon emissions, affected by green technical progress? Based on global patent filings and corporate financial reporting, we analyze corporate green and brown R&D activity and its effects in reducing carbon emissions. We find consistent evidence of limited green R&D by brown companies. Innovating companies with higher carbon emissions engage more in brown R&D and less in green R&D. Despite a steady rise in the share of green R&D, we find little evidence that green innovation reduces the future carbon emissions of 1) innovating firms, 2) non-innovating firms in the same sector, 3) firms in other sectors and, 4) across countries. Direct and indirect emissions are not significantly affected by green innovation across all sectors and around the world, whether in the short term (one year after filing a green patent) or in the medium term (three or five years after filing).

We analyze survey data on ESG beliefs and preferences in a large panel of retail investors linked to administrative data on their investment portfolios. The survey elicits investors’ expectations of long- term ESG equity returns and asks about their motivations, if any, to invest in ESG assets. We doc- ument four facts. First, investors generally expected ESG investments to underperform the market. Between mid-2021 and late-2022, the average expected 10-year annualized return of ESG investments relative to the overall stock market was −1.4%. Second, there is substantial heterogeneity across in- vestors in their ESG return expectations and their motives for ESG investing: 45% of survey respon- dents do not see any reason to invest in ESG, 25% are primarily motivated by ethical considerations, 22% are driven by climate hedging motives, and 7% are motivated by return expectations. Third, there is a link between individuals’ reported ESG investment motives and their actual investment behav- iors, with the highest ESG portfolio holdings among individuals who report ethics-driven investment motives. Fourth, financial considerations matter independently of other investment motives: we find meaningful ESG holdings only for investors who expect these investments to outperform the market, even among those investors who reported that their most important ESG investment motives were ethical or hedging reasons.
