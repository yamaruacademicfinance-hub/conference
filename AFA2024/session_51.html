Interdealer Price Dispersion
Authors: Andrea Eisfeldt (University of California-Los Angeles), Bernard Herskovic (University of California-Los Angeles), Shuo Liu (Tsinghua University)

Intermediation capacity varies across dealers and, as a result, misallocation of credit risk reduces the risk-bearing capacity of the dealer sector and increases effective market-level risk aversion. When the efficient reallocation of credit risk within the dealer sector is impaired, interdealer price dispersion increases. Empirically, interdealer price dispersion is a strong determinant of yield spread changes. When interdealer price dispersion is high, bond prices are low. Interdealer price dispersion explains a substantial portion of bond yield spread changes, the cross-section of bond returns, and the basis between yield spread changes and changes in fair-value spreads. We conclude that frictions within the dealer sector reduce the risk-bearing capacity of intermediaries and are thus crucial for intermediary bond pricing.

The Financial Premium
Authors: Jens Dick-nielsen (Copenhagen Business School), Peter Feldhutter (Copenhagen Business School), David Lando (Copenhagen Business School)
📄 PDF

金融企業が発行する債券は、同じ格付けの産業企業が発行する債券よりもスプレッドが高いことを示し、この差を金融プレミアムと呼ぶ。1987年から2020年の期間において、このプレミアムは米国で平均43bpsであり、これは31％高いスプレッドに相当し、このプレミアムは格付けが低いほど、また金融危機において高い。さらに、プレミアムはシステミック・リスクの尺度と関連し、経済活動を予測する。すなわち、銀行は社債（ローン）のポートフォリオを分散して保有しており、したがって銀行債は個々の社債よりもシステミック・リスクを反映している。

We show that bonds issued by financial firms have higher spreads than bonds issued by industrial firms with the same rating and we denote this difference the financial premium. During the period 1987-2020 the premium was on average 43bps in the U.S. corresponding to a 31% higher spread and the premium is higher for lower ratings and in financial crises. Furthermore, the premium relates to measures of systemic risk and predicts economic activity. We derive a model that explains the empirical results: banks hold a diversified portfolio of corporate bonds (loans) and bank bonds therefore reflect more systematic risk than the individual corporate bonds.

Voter-induced Municipal Credit Risk
Authors: Brent Ambrose (Pennsylvania State University), Matt Gustafson (Pennsylvania State University), Maxence Valentin (Cornell University), Zihan Ye (University of Tennessee-Knoxville)
📄 PDF

住民の公共財コストを暗黙のうちに上昇させる識別ショックとして、減税・雇用法を用いて、住民の公共財への関与が地方政府の借入コストにどのような影響を与えるかを検証する。その結果、住民が平均的な処理強度を負担している管轄区域が発行する地方債の利回りが11～16ベーシスポイント上昇したことを、差分法（difference-in-differences）の枠組みで示す。この効果は、税制改正や地方債発行により厳格な有権者の承認を必要とする法域においてより大きいため、我々の調査結果は、地方債の信用リスクを決定する際に有権者が誘発したプレミアムを示す証拠となる。

We examine how residents' involvement in public finance impacts local government borrowing costs by using the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act as an identifying shock that implicitly raised the cost of public goods for residents. We show in a difference-in-differences framework that yields of municipal bonds issued by jurisdictions whose residents bear the average treatment intensity rose by 11 to 16 basis points. Because the effects are larger in jurisdictions that require more stringent voter approval for tax changes or municipal bond issuance, our findings offer evidence of a voter-induced premium in determining municipal bond credit risk.
