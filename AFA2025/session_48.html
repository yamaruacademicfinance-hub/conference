Daily Momentum and New Investors in an Emerging Stock Market
Authors: Zhenyu Gao (Chinese University of Hong Kong), Wenxi Jiang (Chinese University of Hong Kong), Wei Xiong (Shenzhen Stock Exchange), Wei Xiong (Princeton University)
📄 PDF

中国株式市場では、個人投資家が支配的であるにもかかわらず、週次および月次リターンには価格のモメンタムが顕著に見られない。本研究では、日次リターンに価格のモメンタムが存在することを明らかにし、投資家間の取引の異質性を体系的に分析することで、日次モメンタムと新規投資家の注目度や取引活動との関連性を明らかにした。さらに、我々の発見は、他の様々な新興市場における日次価格のモメンタムの存在を示しており、先進国における相対的な希少性とは対照的である。

Despite the dominance of retail investors in the Chinese stock market, there’s a conspicuous absence of price momentum in weekly and monthly returns. This study uncovers the presence of price momentum in daily returns and, through a systematic analysis of trading heterogeneity among investors, links daily momentum to the attention and trading activities of new investors—a phenomenon particularly significant in emerging stock markets. Furthermore, our findings indicate the existence of daily price momentum in various other emerging markets, contrasting with its relative scarcity in developed ones.

The financial press is a conduit for popular narratives that reflect collective memory about historical events. Some collective memories relate to major stock market crashes, and investors may rely on associated narratives, or “crash narratives,” to inform current beliefs and choices. Using recent advances in computational linguistics, we develop a higher-order measure of narrativity based on newspaper articles that appear following major crashes. We provide evidence that crash narratives propagate broadly once they appear in news articles, and significantly explain predictive variation in market volatility. We exploit investor heterogeneity using survey data to distinguish the effects of narrativity and fundamental conditions and find consistent evidence. Finally, we develop a measure of pure narrativity to examine when financial press is more likely to employ narratives.

What Drives Very Long-Run Cash Flow Expectations?
Authors: Paul Decaire (Arizona State University), Marius Guenzel (University of Pennsylvania)
📄 PDF

企業のキャッシュ・フローをはるか将来まで予測することは、コーポレート・ファイナンスや資産価格決定において中心的な役割を果たす。予測者の身元に関する情報を含み、予測者の詳細な個人的背景を収集することを可能にする、プロの予測者による企業の最終成長率（TGR）に関する大規模かつ新規のデータセットを用いて、我々は重要な事実を確立し、彼らの（超）長期的なキャッシュフロー期待のドライバーを特定する。第一に、TGR予想には、IBESの長期成長（LTG）予想のような、3～5年の短い予測ホライズンを捉える他の予測系列とは異なる経済情報が含まれている。第二に、TGR予想は、実現された長期的な企業成長を強く頑健に予測する。第三に、企業のライフサイクルモデルと一致して、TGR予想は企業の年齢とともに低下する。第四に、TGRのばらつきの主因が国や産業であるような非常に成熟した企業を除き、TGR予想には予測者間の大きな持続的な異質性が存在する。最後に、他の設定（例えば、Giglio et al.2021）と比較すると、予測者の属性と経歴がTGR期待値の持続的な異質性の大部分を説明している。

Expectations of firms' cash flows far into the future are central to corporate finance and asset pricing. Using a large, novel dataset on professional forecasters' terminal growth rates (TGR) of firms that includes information about forecaster identities, allowing us to gather detailed personal backgrounds of forecasters, we establish key facts and identify drivers of their (very) long-run cash flow expectations. First, TGR expectations contain distinct economic information relative to other forecasting series, such as IBES long-term growth (LTG) forecasts which capture shorter, 3-5-year forecast horizons. Second, TGR expectations strongly and robustly predict realized long-run firm growth. Third, consistent with firm life cycle models, TGR expectations decline with firm age. Fourth, with the exception of very mature firms where a firm's country and industry primarily account for the variation in TGRs, there exists large, persistent heterogeneity in TGR expectations across forecasters. Finally, compared to other settings (e.g., Giglio et al. 2021), forecaster demographics and backgrounds explain a larger part of the persistent heterogeneity in TGR expectations.
