One in seven Americans carry medical debt, with $88 billion reported on consumer credit reports. In April 2023, the three major credit bureaus stopped reporting medical debts below $500. We study the effects of this information deletion on consumer credit access and financial health. Using a machine learning model, we show that small medical debts are not meaningfully predictive of defaults, suggesting their deletion should have minimal effect on lending decisions. We test this prediction using two complementary research designs. First, a regression discontinuity analysis comparing individuals just above and below the $500 threshold finds no direct benefits from the information deletion, ruling out small changes in credit access and financial health. Second, to assess potential indirect effects, we classify consumers based on whether their predicted probability of default increases or decreases when medical debts are deleted. A difference-in-differences analysis comparing these groups before and after the 2023 policy change reveals no evidence of negative spillover effects. Finally, we show that larger medical debts (≥ $500) are also not meaningfully predictive of default, suggesting that eliminating medical debts entirely from credit reports, as planned under a January 2025 decision by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, is unlikely to affect credit outcomes.

The Welfare Benefits of Pay-As-You-Go Financing
Authors: Brett Green (Washington University in St. Louis), Renping Li (Tulane University), David Sraer (University of California-Berkeley), Paul Gertler (University of California-Berkeley)
📄 PDF

ペイ・アズ・ユー・ゴー（Pay-as-you-go）ファイナンスは、特に中低所得国（LMICs）において、最近一般的なクレジット形態となった新しい契約である。PAYGoファイナンスはロックアウト技術に依存しており、借り手が支払いを怠った場合、貸し手は遠隔操作で担保のフロー・ベネフィットを無効にすることができる。本稿では、PAYGoファイナンスの厚生への影響を定量化する。我々は、消費者の動的構造モデルを開発し、スマートフォンのPAYGoファイナンスを提供するフィンテックレンダーによって実施されたマルチアーム、大規模な価格設定実験を使ってモデルを推定する。我々は、PAYGoファイナンスへのアクセスによる厚生利得は、貸し手にとって高い収益性を維持しながら、所得の3.4％の増加に相当することを発見した。厚生上の利益は、低リスクの消費者と所得分布の中位にいる消費者ほど大きい。合理的な仮定の下では、PAYGo融資は、最もリスクの高い消費者を除く全ての消費者について、従来の有担保融資を上回る。契約設計を検討し、厚生をさらに改善するPAYGo契約のバリエーションを特定する。

Pay-as-you-go (PAYGo) financing is a novel contract that has recently become a popular form of credit, especially in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs). PAYGo financing relies on lockout technology that enables the lender to remotely disable the flow benefits of collateral when the borrower misses payments. This paper quantifies the welfare implications of PAYGo financing. We develop a dynamic structural model of consumers and estimate the model using a multi-arm, large scale pricing experiment conducted by a fintech lender that offers PAYGo financing for smartphones. We find that the welfare gains from access to PAYGo financing are equivalent to a 3.4% increase in income while remaining highly profitable for the lender. The welfare gains are larger for low-risk consumers and consumers in the middle of the income distribution. Under reasonable assumptions, PAYGo financing outperforms traditional secured loans for all but the riskiest consumers. We explore contract design and identify variations of the PAYGo contract that further improve welfare.

How Do Crypto Flows Finance Slavery? The Economics of Pig Butchering
Authors: John Griffin (University of Texas-Austin), Kevin Mei (University of Texas-Austin)
📄 PDF

ブロックチェーンアドレスを通じて暗号の流れを追跡し、詐欺師が活動を難読化するためによく使う手法を明らかにした。犯人は主要な暗号取引所と自由にやり取りしており、米国や欧州の投資家がよく利用する取引所に年間98,000件以上の少額の信頼構築のための誘引金を送っている。資金は、透明性は低いが大規模な取引所を通じて、主にテザーでイーサリアムネットワークから大量に流出する。犯罪企業 は取引手数料として約33ベーシスポイントを支払い、2021年から2023年の間に年間278億ドルを疑わしい取引所の預金口座に移し、その中には欧米の取引所から送られた年間56億ドルも含まれています。我々の調査結果は、「評判の良い」暗号業界の多くのアクターがいかに犯罪資金の流れを促進しているかを浮き彫りにしている。

Through blockchain addresses, we trace crypto flows and uncover methods commonly used by scammers to obfuscate their activities. The perpetrators interact freely with major crypto exchanges, sending over 98,000 small trust-building inducement payments annually to exchanges commonly used by U.S. and European investors. Funds exit the Ethereum network in large quantities, mostly in Tether, through less transparent but large exchanges. Criminal enterprises pay approximately 33 basis points in transaction fees and moved $27.8 billion annually into suspicious exchange deposit accounts between 2021-2023, including $5.6 billion annually sent from Western exchanges. Our findings highlight how many actors in the “reputable” crypto industry facilitate criminal capital flows.

Housing and Fertility
Authors: Bernardus Doornik (Central Bank of Brazil), Dimas Fazio (National University of Singapore), Tarun Ramadorai (Imperial College London), Janis Skrastins (Washington University in St. Louis)
📄 PDF

本稿では、ブラジルの住宅融資抽選による無作為変動を用いて、住宅へのアクセスが出生率に与える影響を検証する。20～25歳では、住宅を取得することで子どもを持つ確率が平均で32％、子どもの数が33％増加し、40歳以上では出生率の増加は見られない。20歳の場合、30歳で住宅を取得した場合と比較して、すぐに住宅を取得した場合の方が、生涯出生率が2倍増加する。犯罪率が低く、一人当たり所得が高く、持ち家率が高い地域に転居することで、子育てに有利な条件が整う。出生率の増加は、住宅の質が低く、収入に対する家賃負担が大きく、世帯収入が低く、女性の所得シェアが低い地域の世帯ほど強い。これらの結果は、住宅クレジットや物理的スペースの制約を緩和することで、出生率が大幅に上昇することを示唆している。

This paper examines the impact of access to housing on fertility rates using random variation from housing credit lotteries in Brazil. For 20-25-year-olds, we find that obtaining housing increases the average probability of having a child by 32% and the number of children by 33%, with no increase in fertility for people above age 40. The completed lifetime fertility increase for a 20-year-old is twice as large from obtaining housing immediately compared to obtaining it at age 30. Individuals relocate to areas with lower crime rates, higher per capita income, and higher home-ownership rates, creating more favorable conditions for raising children. The increase in fertility is stronger for households in areas with lower quality housing, greater rental expenses relative to income, and those with lower household income and lower female income share. These results suggest that easing housing credit and physical space constraints can significantly increase fertility.
