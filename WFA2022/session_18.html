ESG分離の定量的な影響を評価する。事業売却が影響を与えるためには、影響を受ける企業の資本コストを変化させる必要がある。(1)社会的責任資本の割合、(2)対象企業の割合、(3)対象企業と他の株式市場の相関、の3つのインプットの関数として資本コストの変化に関する簡単な式を導出した。

We evaluate the quantitative impact of ESG divestitures. For divestitures to have impact they must change the cost of capital of affected firms. We derive a simple expression for the change in the cost of capital as a function of three inputs: (1) the fraction of socially conscious capital, (2) the fraction of targeted firms in the economy and (3) the correlation between the targeted firms and the rest of the stock market.

Crowded Ratings: Clientele Effects in the Corporate Bond Market
Authors: Francisco Gomes (London Business School), Ryan Lewis (University of Colorado), Jordan Nickerson (University of Washington)
📄 PDF

本論文は、金融制約の緩和が物理的資本の質を高め、その結果、生産性も高めるという新しい証拠を提供する。インドにおける新しいプロジェクトレベルの投資データセットであるCapExを使用し、プロジェクトコスト、企業に追加された能力、投資の製品カテゴリに関するデータを用いている。物理的資本の質は、プロジェクトコストを追加設備容量で割った単位投資コスト（UIC）を用いて測定した。UICは企業間で大きなばらつきがあり、生産性やアウトプットの質との関連性が高いことが分かった。しかし、質の高い物理的資本は高価であり、十分な内部資金がなければ投資することができない。我々は、Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRT)の設立という政策が、インドの各州における対外債務調達へのアクセスに時差を生じさせていることを研究した。その結果、処理された州の企業は借入と投資を増やし、投資の増加はすべてUICの増加によるものであり、追加能力の増加によるものではないことが分かった。さらに、処理された企業は生産性と生産物の質を向上させた。これは、より大きな資金調達へのアクセスによって誘発されたUICの向上が、企業の生産性と生産物の質を向上させたという仮説と一致している。DRT設置の効果は、外部資金への依存度が高い企業や品質差別化の余地が大きい産業でより強く、この仮説はさらに支持される結果となった。また、他のチャネルが生産性やアウトプットの質の向上を完全に説明するわけではないことを、利用可能な証拠が示している。本稿は、物理的資本の質が生産性とアウトプットの質の重要な決定要因であり、企業が物理的資本の質を選択するかどうかは資金調達の可用性に依存することを明らかにした。∗

This paper provides novel evidence that reduced financial constraints increase physical capital quality and, consequently, productivity. We use a novel project level investment dataset from India, CapEx, with data on project cost, capacity added to the firm, and investment's product category. We measure physical capital quality using Unit Investment Cost (UIC), defined as the project cost divided by the additional capacity. We find UIC displays significant variation across firms and is substantially associated with productivity and output quality. However, higher quality physical capital is more expensive, and without sufficient internal funds, firms cannot invest in them. We study a policy, the establishment of Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRT), which has generated staggered variation in access to external debt financing across different Indian states. We find that firms in treated states borrowed and invested more with all the increased investment coming from an increase in UIC and not from increased additional capacity. Furthermore, treated firms increased productivity and output quality, consistent with the hypothesis that a higher UIC induced by greater access to finance increased firm productivity and output quality. The effect of DRTs establishment is stronger in firms that rely more on external financing and industries with more scope for quality differentiation, a result which further supports this hypothesis. Available evidence suggests that other channels do not completely explain the increased productivity and output quality. Overall, this paper finds physical capital quality is an important determinant of productivity and output quality, and a firm's choice of physical capital quality depends on the availability of financing. ∗

Green Image in Supply Chains: Selective Disclosure of Corporate Suppliers Yilin Shi CUHK Business School, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, ylshi@link.cuhk.edu.hk Jing Wu CUHK Business School, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, jingwu@cuhk.edu.hk Yu Zhang Guanghua School of Management, Peking University, yuzhang@gsm.pku.edu.cn Perception regarding a customer firm's green image depends not only on itself but also on its known suppliers. This paper provides the first empirical evidence linking the supplier's environmental performance and supply chain information disclosure. Specifically, we uncover robust evidence showing that customer firms voluntarily disclose relationships with environmentally responsible ('good') suppliers while selectively not disclosing relationships with 'bad' suppliers. An instrumental variable approach using temperature volatility worldwide supports a causal interpretation of our finding. Such corporate behavior increases with growing public awareness of climate change and decreases with strengthened regulations on environmental information transparency. This observation is more salient for firms that face greater competition or care more about their brand awareness and for firms that have more significant holdings by institutional investors. Firms that selectively disclose 'green' suppliers experience increases in sales and valuation, suggesting that product consumers and financial markets do not fully take this behavior into account.
